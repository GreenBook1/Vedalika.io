# Course Title: Understanding Light

## Chapter 1: Introduction to Light

### Subheading: What is Light?
~~
Light is a form of electromagnetic radiation that is visible to the human eye. It is responsible for the sense of sight and is a crucial aspect of many natural and artificial phenomena.
~~

### Subheading: Properties of Light
~~
1. **Speed**: Light travels at a speed of approximately \( 3 \times 10^8 \) meters per second in a vacuum.
2. **Wavelength**: Light has a range of wavelengths, visible as different colors in the spectrum.
3. **Frequency**: The frequency of light is inversely proportional to its wavelength.
4. **Reflection and Refraction**: Light can change direction when it bounces off surfaces or passes through different media.
Image: path/to/properties-of-light-image.png
~~

## Chapter 2: Light as an Electromagnetic Wave

### Subheading: The Wave Nature of Light
~~
Light behaves as an electromagnetic wave and can be described by the wave equation:
~~

Math: \( c = \lambda f \)

~~
Where:
- \( c \) is the speed of light,
- \( \lambda \) is the wavelength, and
- \( f \) is the frequency.
~~

~~
Derivation of the Wave Equation:
The wave equation relates the speed of light to its wavelength and frequency. It is derived from Maxwell's equations, which describe how electric and magnetic fields propagate through space.
~~

### Subheading: Electromagnetic Spectrum
~~
The electromagnetic spectrum encompasses all types of electromagnetic radiation, from gamma rays to radio waves. Light is located between ultraviolet and infrared in this spectrum.
Image: path/to/electromagnetic-spectrum-image.png
~~

## Chapter 3: Refraction and Reflection

### Subheading: Refraction of Light
~~
Refraction occurs when light passes from one medium to another and changes speed, leading to a change in direction. The refractive index \( n \) of a medium is given by:
Math: \( n = \frac{c}{v} \)

Where:
- \( c \) is the speed of light in a vacuum,
- \( v \) is the speed of light in the medium.

Snell's Law describes how light refracts:
~~

Math: \( n_1 \sin \theta_1 = n_2 \sin \theta_2 \)

~~
Where:
- \( n_1 \) and \( n_2 \) are the refractive indices of the two media,
- \( \theta_1 \) and \( \theta_2 \) are the angles of incidence and refraction, respectively.
~~

### Subheading: Reflection of Light
~~
Reflection occurs when light bounces off a surface. The law of reflection states:
Math: \( \theta_i = \theta_r \)

Where:
- \( \theta_i \) is the angle of incidence,
- \( \theta_r \) is the angle of reflection.

Example: A mirror reflects light at the same angle it strikes the surface.
Image: path/to/reflection-of-light-image.png
~~

## Chapter 4: Lenses and Optics

### Subheading: Lenses and their Properties
~~
Lenses bend light to form images. There are two main types of lenses:
1. **Convex Lenses**: Converge light rays to a point.
2. **Concave Lenses**: Diverge light rays.

The lens formula is:
Math: \( \frac{1}{f} = \frac{1}{v} - \frac{1}{u} \)

Where:
- \( f \) is the focal length,
- \( v \) is the image distance,
- \( u \) is the object distance.
Image: path/to/lenses-properties-image.png
~~

### Subheading: Optical Instruments
~~
Optical instruments like microscopes and telescopes use lenses to magnify objects. The magnification \( M \) of a lens system is given by:
~~

Math: \( M = \frac{v}{u} \)

Where:
- \( v \) is the image distance,
- \( u \) is the object distance.

## Chapter 5: Advanced Topics

### Subheading: Interference and Diffraction
~~
Interference occurs when two or more light waves overlap and combine. Diffraction involves the bending of light around obstacles. Both phenomena can be observed in various experiments such as the double-slit experiment.
Image: path/to/interference-diffraction-image.png
~~

### Subheading: Polarization of Light
~~
Polarization refers to the orientation of light waves in a specific direction. Polarized sunglasses, for example, reduce glare by blocking horizontally polarized light.
